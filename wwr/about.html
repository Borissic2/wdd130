History

Wild Wave Rafting was founded in 1995 by adventure enthusiasts Sarah Thompson and Jack Carter in Rivertown, along the Rapids River. Starting with a few rafts and a passion for whitewater rafting, they quickly built a reputation for thrilling and safe river adventures. By the early 2000s, the company expanded its offerings to include guided tours, multi-day expeditions, and eco-friendly practices, gaining recognition in the adventure tourism industry. Wild Wave Rafting introduced comprehensive safety training for their guides and invested in high-quality equipment, ensuring the best possible experience for their clients.

Today, Wild Wave Rafting is a leader in the field, known for its commitment to safety, customer satisfaction, and environmental stewardship. The company operates on multiple rivers across the country and offers a diverse range of activities, including kayaking, stand-up paddleboarding, and river hiking. They continue to innovate with new programs and initiatives, such as educational programs on river ecology and conservation, inspiring a lifelong love for nature and adventure. With plans to expand internationally, Wild Wave Rafting aims to bring their unique brand of eco-conscious tourism to rivers around the world.
